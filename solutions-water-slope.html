01 / Industry Pain Points

Why Reservoir Slopes Fail

Reservoir slopes face a unique combination of hydrological and geotechnical stresses. Rapid water level changes, prolonged saturation, and bank erosion create failure conditions that traditional inspection cannot adequately monitor.

Rapid Water Level Drawdown

Fast reservoir drawdown reduces confining pressure on saturated slopes faster than pore pressure can dissipate, dramatically increasing instability risk in the fluctuation zone.

Bank Erosion & Undercutting

Wave action and current erosion progressively undercut slope toes. Periodic surveys miss the cumulative erosion that precedes sudden bank collapse events.

Saturated Soil Instability

Prolonged high water levels saturate slope soils, reducing shear strength. Without continuous pore pressure monitoring, the critical saturation threshold is invisible until failure.

Fragmented Data Silos

Water level, displacement, and pore pressure data collected by separate systems prevents holistic slope risk assessment and delays critical early warning decisions.

02 / Solution Overview

Integrated Monitoring Architecture

Midu's reservoir slope solution fuses GNSS displacement arrays, in-place inclinometers, water level gauges, and piezometers into a single real-time intelligence platform.

Sector View W-01 Reservoir Slope Zone

The system deploys GNSS rover stations across the slope face and crest, providing continuous 3D displacement vectors at sub-millimeter resolution. In-place inclinometers profile lateral movement at depth, detecting slip surface development before surface expression occurs.

Water level gauges correlate reservoir fluctuation events with displacement and pore pressure data — enabling predictive risk assessment during drawdown operations.
