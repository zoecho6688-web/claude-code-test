01 / Project Overview

Project Overview

The Democratic Republic of the Congo project involved deploying a comprehensive 24/7 automated GNSS displacement monitoring network across one of Central Africa's largest open-pit copper mining operations. The mine spans over 8 km² of active extraction area with slopes reaching heights of 340 meters, presenting significant geotechnical risk requiring continuous surveillance.

Medo's team conducted a full site survey and risk zoning assessment prior to installation, identifying 14 high-priority monitoring zones along the eastern and northern pit walls. A total of 28 GNSS rover stations were installed in conjunction with 3 reference base stations, creating a dense displacement monitoring mesh capable of detecting sub-millimeter surface movements across the entire slope face.

All data streams are transmitted via the IoT gateway network to Medo's cloud platform in real time, where automated processing and multi-tier alerting algorithms continuously evaluate slope stability against pre-defined safety thresholds. The system operates fully unattended, with solar and battery backup ensuring continuous operation through the region's wet and dry seasons.

02 / Project Features

Project Highlights

This deployment presented a unique combination of technical and logistical challenges that demanded purpose-built solutions across hardware, communications infrastructure, and software architecture. The following key characteristics defined the approach and differentiated it from standard monitoring installations.

01

Extreme Remote Environment Deployment

The mine site is located over 180 km from the nearest urban infrastructure center. All hardware — including GNSS receivers, IoT gateways, solar panels, and battery banks — was engineered for tool-free installation by local mine personnel following remote commissioning guidance from Medo's engineering team. IP67-rated enclosures withstand the region's tropical rainfall and dust exposure without maintenance intervention.

02

Multi-Path Satellite Uplink Architecture

Given the absence of reliable 4G coverage across the pit floor, the system employs a hybrid uplink strategy combining local LoRa mesh networking between sensor nodes and primary/backup satellite uplinks at the gateway level. This ensures zero data loss even during planned LTE outages or adverse weather events that temporarily affect ground-level communications.

03

Dynamic Blasting Interference Suppression

Active open-pit mines conduct daily blasting operations that generate significant ground vibration and temporary GNSS signal interference. The monitoring software incorporates a blasting event mask protocol — operators log blast timing via the platform, and the algorithm automatically excludes contaminated measurement windows while maintaining displacement trend continuity across the interruption, preventing false positive alerts.

04

Tiered Alert Distribution Across Language Groups

The mine operates with engineering staff communicating in English, French, and Lingala. Alert notifications are configured to route language-appropriate SMS and email messages to relevant personnel based on role and location zone. Level-1 attention alerts reach zone supervisors; Level-2 warning alerts escalate to shift engineers; Level-3 emergency alerts simultaneously notify site management, the corporate safety team, and Medo's 24/7 remote monitoring center.

03 / Outcomes

Outcomes & Impact

Early Warning
23 days

Lead time before a critical displacement event reached visible threshold — allowing controlled drawdown and equipment evacuation with zero injuries.

Downtime Prevented
$4.2M

Estimated production loss and remediation cost avoided by the early slope stabilization intervention triggered by the system's Level-2 alert.

System Uptime
99.7%

Across 18 months of continuous operation including two wet seasons, achieving an availability rate that exceeded contractual SLA requirements by 1.2%.

Alert Accuracy
0

False positive emergency alerts issued in the first 18 months of operation, attributed to the blasting interference suppression and multi-sensor confirmation logic.

Beyond the immediate safety outcomes, the project demonstrated that high-precision GNSS monitoring infrastructure can be successfully deployed and sustained in sub-Saharan African mining environments with minimal on-site technical expertise requirements. The mine's geotechnical team reported a significant shift in operational confidence — moving from reactive, inspection-based slope management to proactive, data-driven decision-making supported by continuous displacement trend analysis.

The success of this deployment has since informed the design standard for Medo's subsequent African mining projects, particularly with respect to communications redundancy architecture and multi-language alert routing, both of which have been adopted as platform defaults for all international project configurations.
