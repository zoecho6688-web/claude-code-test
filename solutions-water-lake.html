01 / Industry Pain Points

Why River & Lake Monitoring Is Critical

Rivers and lakes face mounting pressure from agricultural runoff, industrial discharge, and climate-driven temperature shifts. Traditional sampling methods are too slow and too sparse to catch fast-moving threats.

Manual Sampling Inefficiency

Weekly or monthly grab samples miss the dynamic fluctuations in pH, dissolved oxygen, and nutrients that occur between visits — leaving regulators and operators blind to real conditions.

Slow Response to Pollution Incidents

Industrial spills and agricultural runoff events can travel kilometers downstream before detection. Without continuous sensing, response teams arrive after the damage is done.

Unpredictable Algae Bloom (水华) Outbreaks

Algae blooms develop rapidly when nutrient levels and temperature align. Without trend analysis, the warning window is too short to protect drinking water intakes and aquatic ecosystems.

Fragmented Environmental Data

Water quality, flow rate, and meteorological data collected by separate agencies in incompatible formats prevents the holistic analysis needed for effective watershed management.
