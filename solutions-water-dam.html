01 / Industry Pain Points

Why Reservoir Dams Fail

Reservoir dams face persistent threats from internal seepage, foundation deformation, and reservoir bank instability — risks that manual inspection cannot adequately detect in time.

Internal Seepage (渗透)

Seepage through the dam body or foundation builds pore pressure invisibly. Without continuous piezometric monitoring, piping failure can develop undetected until catastrophic breach occurs.

Dam Foundation Deformation (基础形变)

Differential settlement and foundation creep under sustained hydraulic load can compromise structural integrity. Early displacement detection is critical to prevent progressive failure.

Reservoir Bank Stability

Fluctuating reservoir water levels destabilize surrounding slopes and banks. Rapid drawdown in particular creates high pore pressure gradients that can trigger large-scale bank collapses.

Primary Case Study · 2023

Southwest Reservoir Dam
Seepage Safety Project

Background

A large earth-rockfill dam in Southwest China with a 120m-high dam body and 4.2 km² reservoir surface. Following detection of increased seepage flow at the drainage gallery, the operator commissioned a comprehensive automated monitoring system.

Deployment

Midu deployed 12 GNSS stations, 24 piezometers at 3 depth levels, 4 V-notch weirs at drainage outlets, 6 water level gauges, and 3 rain gauges across the dam body and reservoir banks. All sensors integrated via LoRaWAN into the Midu cloud platform.

Outcome

During the following flood season, the system detected a seepage anomaly in the right abutment zone 11 days before any visible signs appeared. Automated Level-2 alerts triggered a controlled drawdown protocol — preventing a potential piping failure with zero accidents recorded throughout the monitoring period.
