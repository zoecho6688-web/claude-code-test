01 / Critical Risks

High-Velocity Disaster Dynamics

Flash floods and debris flows are triggered in seconds. Conventional contact sensing is often destroyed before alerts can be fired, requiring a more resilient, non-contact approach.

Sudden Onset

Mountain torrents can surge within 15-30 minutes of extreme rainfall, leaving zero margin for manual human reporting or relay.

Destructive Impact

Debris flows carry immense kinetic energy (rocks and mud), which obliterates traditional in-stream sensors during the initial wave.

Remote 'Blind' Zones

Hazardous gullies often lack cellular coverage and power access, requiring ultra-low power and satellite transmission protocols.

Complex Thresholds

Correlation between rainfall intensity and soil saturation is non-linear, requiring AI-driven threshold logic to avoid false alarms.

02 / Solution Chain

The 'Golden' Detection Protocol

We deploy a multi-layered sensing chain that monitors disaster development from trigger to surge, gaining vital seconds for downstream evacuation.

Phase 01: Trigger

Rainfall Correlation

Automated pluviometers track cumulative rainfall and intensity, establishing the disaster-triggering baseline model for specific basins.

Phase 02: Early Warning

Infrasound Detection

Capturing the unique low-frequency 'roar' of debris flows 5-15 minutes before impact, allowing for early high-level emergency alerts.

Phase 03: Confirmation

Radar Level Analysis

Non-contact radar sensing confirms flow velocity and mud level rise for final automated sirens and SMS broadcasting.
