01 / Industry Pain Points

Why Reservoir Bank Slopes Fail

Reservoir bank slopes are inherently complex due to the **non-homogeneous and anisotropic nature** of rock masses. The dynamic interaction between fluctuating water levels and shifting hydrogeological conditions creates invisible risks that traditional monitoring fails to capture.

Geomechanical Heterogeneity

Rock masses exhibit high levels of heterogeneity and anisotropy. This inherent complexity makes it nearly impossible to predict stress distribution and potential failure planes using only surface-level inspections.

Hydraulic Gradient Shifts

Reservoir impoundment raises groundwater tables and local erosion base levels. These drastic shifts in hydrogeological conditions destabilize the bank, triggering rapid and unforeseen slope deformation.

Invisible Stability Decay

Fluctuating water levels significantly alter pore water pressure and effective stress within the slope. Without real-time, deep-seated sensing, these critical stability fluctuations remain undetected until a catastrophic event.

02 / Solution Overview

From Sensor to Warning: The Data Flow

Medo's tailings solution creates an unbroken chain from field instrumentation to real-time decision support — five layers, zero gaps.

Step 01

On-site Survey & Site Assessment

Focusing on the unique challenges of phosphogypsum storage, our engineers perform rigorous onsite assessments of stack morphology and seepage risks. We prioritize environmental safety by evaluating leachate impact and chemical erosion zones. This process yields critical insights, including groundwater vulnerability reports and specialized sensor deployment strategies, ensuring a tailored monitoring architecture that withstands harsh chemical environments while safeguarding the surrounding ecosystem.

Step 02

Hardware & Integrated Monitoring Stations Development

Based on the survey results, we deploy a network of all-in-one integrated monitoring stations at critical locations across the site. Each station combines a high-precision GNSS receiver with a suite of environmental and structural sensors — measuring displacement, seepage, pore water pressure, rainfall, and more. Ruggedised enclosures ensure continuous operation in harsh outdoor conditions.

Step 03

Data Transmission & GNSS Processing Platform

Raw sensor data is securely transmitted to the cloud-based MEDO computation platform, AMS, via satellite, 4G, or 5G links — whichever provides the most reliable coverage for the site. MEDO performs real-time GNSS baseline processing, multi-sensor fusion, and precision deformation calculation, converting raw signals into high-accuracy displacement vectors and trend indicators.

Step 04

Intelligent Data Analytics & Remote Device Management

MDT Platform streamlines high-precision data acquisition specifically for phosphogypsum stacks. By integrating comprehensive remote device management, the platform allows for real-time monitoring of sensor health, battery levels, and signal strength, ensuring stable performance. By data visualization, the platform empowers users with instantaneous updates on stack integrity, seepage, and hardware status, ensuring the facility’s safety remains visible, manageable, and under complete control.

Step 05

Full-Cycle Lifecycle Management and Decision Support

Powered by MD-NET and MD LINK, this platform integrates project lifecycle management with advanced geomechanical intelligence. Through intuitive data visualization, we assist management units in implementing timely emergency protocols. Our commitment to continuous system evolution ensures that your facility remains protected against potential disasters, minimizing the risk of casualties and environmental damage through every stage of operation.

03 / Core Advantages

Mastering Reservoir Bank Stability

Standard monitoring fails to account for the dynamic interaction between water and rock. Our solution is engineered to quantify the invisible shifts in bank stability caused by fluctuating reservoir levels and complex hydrogeological conditions.

01

Integrated Surface-to-Deep Sensing

Combines high-precision GNSS with deep-seated MEMS inclinometers. This dual-layer approach overcomes the challenges of rock mass anisotropy, capturing both subtle surface movements and internal shear plane development.

02

Hydraulic-Mechanical Linkage Analysis

Real-time correlation between reservoir water levels and internal pore water pressure. Our system quantifies how impoundment and drawdown cycles affect the effective stress within the slope, providing a scientific basis for stability assessment.

03

Dynamic Stability Predictive Modeling

Moves beyond simple thresholds by using advanced geomechanical models. The system predicts the "Time-to-Failure" based on cumulative deformation trends and shifting phreatic lines, enabling proactive rather than reactive management.

04

Unified Reservoir Management Dashboard

A single, intuitive platform that correlates weather data, hydrological fluctuations, and slope displacement. Provides stakeholders with a holistic view of the reservoir’s health, from individual slopes to the entire bank perimeter.
