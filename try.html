01 / Industry Pain Points

Why Reservoirs Are High-Risk

Underground mining creates vast hidden voids that destabilize the rock mass above. Surface subsidence develops slowly — then suddenly — with devastating consequences for infrastructure and communities.

Sudden Void Collapse

Goaf roofs can fail without warning. Traditional inspection cannot detect the micro-deformation signals that precede catastrophic collapse events.

Progressive Surface Subsidence

Millimeter-scale surface settlement accumulates over months. Periodic survey methods miss the early displacement trends that signal accelerating subsidence.

Delayed Community Warning

Without automated alerting, subsidence events affecting roads, buildings, and pipelines are only discovered after visible damage — long after the safe evacuation window has closed.

Fragmented Monitoring Data

Surface GNSS, InSAR, and underground stress data collected in isolation prevents holistic goaf risk assessment and delays critical safety decisions.
