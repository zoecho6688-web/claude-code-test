01 / Industry Pain Points

The Embankment Challenge

River embankments and levees face relentless hydraulic pressure, seasonal flood cycles, and hidden internal erosion. Failures are often sudden and catastrophic — demanding continuous, automated monitoring year-round.

Seepage & Piping (管涌)

Internal seepage channels can develop silently within embankment fill. Piping erosion progresses rapidly once initiated, often leading to sudden structural breach with little surface warning.

Riverbank Erosion

Flood currents and wave action progressively undercut embankment toes. Scour and surface erosion weaken slope geometry and accelerate deformation under high-water conditions.

Overtopping Risks

Extreme rainfall and upstream surge events can push water levels beyond design crest elevations. Real-time water level and crest settlement data are essential for timely flood response.

Fragmented Monitoring Data

Piezometer, settlement, and water level data collected in isolation prevents holistic embankment risk assessment and delays critical flood-season decisions.

02 / Solution Overview

From Sensor to Warning: The Data Flow

Medo's embankment solution creates an unbroken chain from field instrumentation to real-time decision support — three layers, zero gaps.

Step 01

Sensing — Surface GNSS + Internal Piezometers

GNSS stations monitor surface deformation and crest settlement at millimeter resolution, while embedded piezometers track internal pore water pressure and seepage gradients throughout the embankment body.

Step 02

Telemetry — Smart Terminal

Solar-powered smart terminals aggregate all sensor streams locally, apply pre-processing filters, and transmit via 4G/NB-IoT with low-latency uplinks — maintaining connectivity even during flood-season field conditions.

Step 03

Analysis — Cloud Platform

All data streams converge in Medo's cloud platform — unified dashboards, seepage line visualization, historical trend analysis, and automated three-tier alerts for flood control authorities.
